Flies and Mosquitoes Control

When flies and mosquitoes swarm around the house in huge numbers, they can be a nuisance. Some fly and mosquito species, on the other hand, can be harmful to your health.

House flies, in particular, spread a variety of diseases, including salmonella, dysentery, TB, cholera, and parasitic worms.

Mosquitoes are blamed for the rise of Dengue Fever in this country, but unlike in the tropics, they are unlikely to transfer Malaria. Even if no disease is present, the irritation caused by the bites can be disturbing, especially for children.

Types of Mosquito and Fly Problems

There are thousands of different fly species, however the following are the most typical causes of fly and mosquito problems:

House Flies :
Adults 6-7mm. Female houseflies lay 400-600 eggs, which hatch into maggot larvae, in decomposing materials or faeces. Attracted to any food or organic garbage that has been left out.

Blow Flies or Bottle Flies :
Adults 6-12mm, Usually shiny blue or green, but black or grey is also an option. Significant numbers in a home often signal the presence of a deceased animal (such as a mouse).

Cluster Flies :
Adults 6-10mm. Cluster flies sometimes spend the winter within dwellings (such as attics), and when they emerge in the spring, they can be found in great numbers.

Mosquitoes :
Adults 5mm. Their larvae develop near still water, therefore they prefer to be near it.

What can you do to keep mosquitoes and flies at bay?

It is critical not to attract flies with food sources or sites for fly larvae to thrive for many species of fly, including house flies and bottle flies:

Food should always be covered because flies can transfer diseases by landing on it before we eat it.

Food and liquid spills should be cleaned up right away.

Remove any food debris from beneath the kitchen equipment.

Ensure that all trash can lids are securely fastened.

Cover and contain the compost.

Clean up after your pets:
not only are faeces a great spot for flies to reproduce, but they're also a good area for a fly to land before landing on your meal.

Prevent flies and mosquitoes from breeding in stagnant water

Ensure that drains are clear and flowing.

Bleach drains that appear to be infested with flies.

Make certain that gutters and water gullies are not clogged.

Using a lid, cover the water butts.

Do not allow standing water to accumulate (eg: in watering cans)

In order to devour mosquito and fly larvae, place goldfish in garden ponds.

To keep mosquitoes and flies out of your home, take the following steps

After dusk, close windows and doors or use drapes to block out light.

Install fly screens in windows, particularly around the kitchen and garbage disposal areas.
