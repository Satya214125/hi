Problems Caused by Creepy Crawlies

There are thousands of distinct 'Creepy Crawlies' or 'Casual Intruder' species, but here are a handful of the most prevalent ones:

Centipedes:
Centipedes come in over a hundred different species, all of which have lengthy bodies with anything from 15 to 173 segments. There are one set of legs on each segment. The head of a centipede has a pair of antennae as well as other mouthparts.

The centipede's thorax bears two venous claws. These are employed for both self-defense and capturing and paralysing their food supply.

Centipedes are fast-moving insects that prey on other insects

A bite from a smaller species of centipede, particularly one from the tropics, feels similar to a Bee or Wasp sting in temperate climates, whereas a bite from a larger species of centipede, particularly one from the tropics, is not only extremely painful but also leaves two puncture wounds at the bite site.

Carpet Beetle:
This species occurs in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colours, but they all look like ladybirds and range in colour from black to brown, white, and yellow. Adults are around 2-3mm in length. Adults are not a nuisance in the home because they prefer to dwell outside and feed on nectar and pollen. However, as the summer progresses, they are more likely to enter your home and lay up to 40 eggs at an once in any natural fibres in your home, such as curtains and carpets.

The eggs hatch into larvae in 2 to 3 weeks, and it is these larvae, sometimes known as Woolly Bears, that cause so much damage since they eat any natural fibre, including carpets, soft furniture, and clothing.

Silverfish :
Adults are 10-12mm long, silver grey in colour, and torpedo-shaped, with three bristles on the Abdomen's end. Silverfish prefer damp, warm environments, and they frequently establish themselves in bathrooms and kitchens. They feed on food trash and, while they prefer a starchy diet, they have developed to be able to breakdown cellulose and can inflict serious harm to your paintings and book collections if permitted access.

Earwig :
Adults are 14-23mm in length, dark brown in appearance, and have forceps on the back end. The forceps, which are curled in males and straight in females, are not venomous and will simply nip you. The female lays about 40 eggs per year and raises the young herself, which is remarkable for an insect. Earwigs are more of a nuisance in the garden than in the house since they feast on green greenery and other plants.
