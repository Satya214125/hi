Although there are dozens of distinct kinds of ants, the following are the most frequent in this country:

Garden Ants:Garden ants, or worker ants, are a type of ant that lives in gardens. 4-5mm, although the Queen can be up to 15mm in size. In the spring, queens lay their eggs in nests that are normally made of dirt, but can also be made of brickwork or under bark. Garden ants are drawn to sweet foods and leave a pheromone trail that other ants can follow back to their nests to find the food source.

Pharaoh’s Ants –Pharaoh's Ants have 1.5-2mm long worker ants and a 4–6mm long Queen. The Queen and the workforce are both yellow-brown in colour. Pharaoh's Ants are tropical ants that require artificial heat to thrive and breed. They prefer to live in large heated facilities such as hospitals, hotels, and apartment buildings. This ant's diet consists primarily of rotting foodstuffs, and it contains hazardous bacteria that it picked up while feeding.

Red Fire Ants – Red Fire Ants have workers that are 3mm long and a Queen that can be up to 6mm long. These ants are reddish brown in colour and can live in colonies of 100,000 or more! A Queen leads each colony, laying between 150 and 200 eggs every day. Fire ants dwell in mounds up to 2 feet high, which they normally form in open, sunny areas. Both animals and people get stung by fire ants, and the sting is exceedingly painful.

How to Prevent Ants from Invading

The majority of black or garden ants will only enter your home to forage for food and will be drawn in by sweet or sticky items.

Ascertain that there is no readily available food source in your home by doing the following:

Always cover your food — we have no idea where the ant has gone before it crawls across it!

Food and liquid spills should be cleaned up right away.

Food debris should be cleaned up from under kitchen equipment.

Ensure that all trash can lids are securely fastened. Cover and contain the compost.

Remove your pet's food as soon as it has done eating.

Sealing entry points such as cracks and gaps in door and window frames might help prevent Ants from entering your property.
