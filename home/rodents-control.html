Rodents Control

Rodents Control

Rats are a serious health threat to both humans and pets. Salmonella, Weil's disease, and tuberculosis are among the diseases carried by rats. Fleas, mites, and ticks are also carried by rats, which can induce severe allergic reactions.

Rats in the house or in the yard

It is extremely serious if rats are getting into your home even if they are not entering a direct living area. Any rat problem inside the home must be treated urgently

Rats in the garden and other external areas can also be high risk, particularly in areas used by children or pets. It is important to get rid of rats in the garden to reduce the risk of them trying to enter the home. Special care is needed for properties with integral garages or with dog or cat flaps as rats can use these to get into the home.

Symptoms of a Rat Issue

Because rats are nocturnal and prefer to conceal from humans, the following are common symptoms of a rat infestation in the home:

As rats run around, scratching noises can be heard in the walls or under the floor.

Droppings:
Rats excrete black, tapering droppings measuring 10-14mm in length. Rats leave a distinct ammonia-like odour that is particularly prominent in enclosed spaces such as under cabinets.

Damage :
Rats have continuously growing teeth, which they trim by gnawing on wood and plastic. By gnawing through cables, rats can even start fires.

Torn food packaging :
Rats will rip through food, possibly leaving tooth imprints.

Nests :
Rats use shredded newspaper and fabrics to construct nests in warm, secluded locations. Young rats are frequently found in nests.

Burrows :
Rats dig holes in gardens, especially in compost piles and under sheds. They'll make nests under garden decking as well.

How to Prevent Rats from Invading

The first line of defence against a rat infestation is to ensure that your home is rat-proof. Because young rats may squeeze through openings as small as 1cm, it's critical to seal any gaps and install a bristle strip around doors.

Ultrasonic repellers produce a sound that is unpleasant to rats but not audible to people. Although these may dissuade rats from entering a home in the first place, we do not recommend them for existing infestations because rats can soon become accustomed to the ultrasound. Food should be stored in plastic or metal containers and under cookers and refrigerators should be cleaned on a regular basis to avoid attracting rats. Keep household trash in covered bins, avoid putting meat in compost piles, and use squirrel-proof bird feeders. Rats can emerge from sewers via broken pipes, so make sure all pipework is in good working order.

Rats: How to Get Rid of Them

A rat infestation can swiftly spread if not treated. For rat activity in living areas or if the problem persists, we recommend treatment by one of our service technicians. Keeping rats out of your house and garden

General stores, garden centres, and grocery stores sell a variety of rodent control goods for home use, including rat traps and rat killer (rat poison). To guarantee safe and successful use as well as humane rat control, it is critical to carefully follow the directions. Rat traps are extremely powerful and should only be used where children, pets, and wildlife cannot get them.
